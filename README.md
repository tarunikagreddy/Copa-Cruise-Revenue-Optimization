# 🚢 Copa Cruise Revenue Optimization

---

## 📘 Overview

This project applies revenue management and pricing analytics techniques to optimize Copa Cruise's pricing strategy. Using Excel, quantitative analysis, and demand modeling, the project identifies optimal pricing points and customer segmentation strategies to maximize annual revenue.

The analysis evaluates customer booking behavior, estimates demand sensitivity to price changes, and develops data-driven pricing recommendations to improve revenue performance.

---

## 📂 Repository Contents

- **CopaCruise_Data.xlsx** — Excel workbook containing demand modeling, pricing optimization, segmentation analysis, and revenue projections.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

### 1. Data Preparation

- Analyzed historical booking and pricing data for Copa Cruise events.
- Examined customer booking outcomes across multiple event types, locations, and booking windows.
- Prepared data for demand estimation and pricing analysis.

### 2. Demand Modeling

- Built a logit regression model in Excel to estimate customer booking probability as a function of price.
- Evaluated customer price sensitivity and its impact on expected demand.
- Generated demand estimates to support pricing decisions.

### 3. Revenue Optimization

- Determined the optimal uniform price per customer using demand and revenue projections.
- Compared projected revenue against historical business performance.
- Evaluated pricing strategies to maximize expected annual revenue.

### 4. Segmentation Analysis

- Created segmentation models based on:
  - Event Type
  - Location
  - Booking Window
- Compared customer responsiveness across segments.
- Assessed opportunities for more targeted pricing strategies.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Customer demand varies significantly across event categories, locations, and booking behaviors.
- Segmentation revealed meaningful differences in customer price sensitivity.
- Booking window segmentation (early vs. late bookings) produced the strongest pricing performance.
- Dynamic pricing strategies can outperform a uniform pricing approach by better aligning prices with customer behavior.
